## ⚡ Question (Average Current)

A lightning bolt transfers a charge of 30 Coulombs to the ground in a time of 2 milliseconds.

👉 Find the average current of the lightning bolt.

---

## 🧠 What is this problem about?

This problem is about **electric current**.

Electric current describes how much electric charge flows through a conductor in a certain amount of time.

We are asked to calculate the **average current** produced by the lightning bolt.

---

## 📌 Formula

I = Q / t

---

## ❓ What does this formula mean?

This formula calculates the **average electric current**.

### Meaning of terms:
- **I** → current (Amperes, A)  
- **Q** → electric charge (Coulombs, C)  
- **t** → time (seconds, s)  

---

## ❓ Why do we use this formula?

Current is defined as:

> the amount of charge flowing per unit time.

So:
- more charge → larger current  
- shorter time → larger current  

That is why current is calculated using:

I = Q / t

---

## 🎯 Important Conversion

The time is given in milliseconds.

1 millisecond = 10<sup>-3</sup> seconds

So:

2 ms = 2 × 10<sup>-3</sup> s = 0.002 s

---

## 🔢 Solution

### 1. Known values
- Q = 30 C  
- t = 0.002 s  

---

### 2. Substitute into the formula

I = 30 / 0.002

---

### 3. Calculate

I = 15000 A

---

## ✅ Final Answer

👉 I = 15000 A

---

## 🎤 Short Explanation

> Average current is calculated using I = Q / t.  
> The lightning transfers 30 Coulombs in 0.002 seconds.  
> Therefore, the average current is 15000 amperes.
