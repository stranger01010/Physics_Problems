## 🔋 Question (Capacitors in Parallel)

Two capacitors:

- C₁ = 4 μF  
- C₂ = 6 μF  

are connected in parallel to a 10 V battery.

👉 Find:
1. The total charge stored  
2. The total energy stored  

---

## 🧠 What is this problem about?

This problem is about **capacitors connected in parallel**.

A capacitor stores electric charge and electrical energy.

When capacitors are connected in parallel:
- they have the same voltage  
- their capacitances add together  

---

## 📌 Formula 1 — Equivalent Capacitance

C = C₁ + C₂

---

## 📚 Name of the Formula

This is the **Parallel Capacitor Formula**.

It is used when capacitors are connected side-by-side in an electric circuit.

---

## ❓ Why do we use this formula?

In a parallel connection:
- both capacitors experience the same voltage  
- the total capacitance increases  

So we simply add the capacitances.

---

## 🔢 Step 1 — Calculate Total Capacitance

C = 4 μF + 6 μF

C = 10 μF

---

## ❓ Why do we convert μF to F?

The standard SI unit of capacitance is the **Farad (F)**.

The symbol **μ (micro)** means:

μ = 10<sup>-6</sup>

So:

1 μF = 1 × 10<sup>-6</sup> F

Therefore:

10 μF = 10 × 10<sup>-6</sup> F

We convert to Farads because capacitor formulas use SI units.

---

## 📌 Formula 2 — Charge Stored

Q = C × V

---

## 📚 Name of the Formula

This is the **Capacitor Charge Formula**.

It relates:
- charge (Q)
- capacitance (C)
- voltage (V)

---

## ❓ Why do we use this formula?

A capacitor stores more charge when:
- capacitance is larger  
- voltage is larger  

That is why charge depends on both C and V.

---

## 🔢 Step 2 — Calculate Total Charge

Q = (10 × 10<sup>-6</sup>) × 10

---

## ❓ Why did we multiply by 10?

Because the battery voltage is:

V = 10 V

The formula says:

Q = C × V

So we must multiply:
- capacitance → 10 × 10<sup>-6</sup> F  
by
- voltage → 10 V  

That gives the total stored charge.

---

## 🔢 Continue Calculation

Q = 100 × 10<sup>-6</sup> C

---

## ❓ Why does 100 × 10<sup>-6</sup> become 1 × 10<sup>-4</sup>?

Because:

100 = 10²

So:

100 × 10<sup>-6</sup>
=
10² × 10<sup>-6</sup>

When multiplying powers of 10, we add exponents:

10² × 10<sup>-6</sup> = 10<sup>-4</sup>

Therefore:

Q = 1 × 10<sup>-4</sup> C

---

## 📌 Formula 3 — Energy Stored

E = (1/2) C V²

---

## 📚 Name of the Formula

This is the **Energy Stored in a Capacitor Formula**.

It calculates the electrical energy stored inside a capacitor.

---

## ❓ Why do we use this formula?

A capacitor stores electrical potential energy.

The stored energy depends on:
- capacitance  
- voltage  

Higher voltage means more stored energy because voltage is squared.

---

## 🔢 Step 3 — Calculate Energy

E = (1/2) × (10 × 10<sup>-6</sup>) × (10)²

---

## ❓ Why is the voltage squared?

The energy stored in a capacitor increases rapidly with voltage.

In capacitor physics, stored energy is proportional to V².

That is why the formula contains voltage squared.

---

## ❓ Why does (10)² become 100?

Because:

10² = 10 × 10 = 100

---

## 🔢 Continue Calculation

E = 0.5 × 10 × 10<sup>-6</sup> × 100

---

## ❓ Why does 0.5 × 10 × 100 become 500?

First:

10 × 100 = 1000

Then:

0.5 × 1000 = 500

So:

E = 500 × 10<sup>-6</sup>

---

## ❓ Why does 500 × 10<sup>-6</sup> become 5 × 10<sup>-4</sup>?

Because:

500 = 5 × 10²

So:

500 × 10<sup>-6</sup>
=
5 × 10² × 10<sup>-6</sup>

Add exponents:

10² × 10<sup>-6</sup> = 10<sup>-4</sup>

Therefore:

E = 5 × 10<sup>-4</sup> J

---

## ✅ Final Answers

- Total capacitance: 10 μF  
- Total charge: 1 × 10<sup>-4</sup> C  
- Total energy: 5 × 10<sup>-4</sup> J  

---

## 🎤 Short Explanation

> In a parallel connection, capacitances add together.  
> We convert μF to F because SI units are required in capacitor formulas.  
> Then we use Q = CV to calculate charge and E = (1/2)CV² to calculate stored energy.  
> The total charge is 1 × 10⁻⁴ C and the stored energy is 5 × 10⁻⁴ J.
