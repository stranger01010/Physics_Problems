## ⚡ Question (Current)

Charge flowing through a wire is given by the function:

Q(t) = 5t² + 5

👉 Find the current at t = 3 s.

---

## 🧠 What is this problem about?

This problem is about **electric current**.

Electric current describes how fast electric charge flows through a wire.

The charge changes with time, so we use the relationship between charge and current.

---

## 📌 Formula

I = dQ / dt

---

## 📚 Name of the Formula

This formula is called the **Current Equation** or the **Definition of Electric Current**.

It is commonly used in:
- electric circuits  
- electronics  
- electromagnetism  
- current and charge analysis  

---

## ❓ What does this formula mean?

This formula states that current is the **rate of change of charge with respect to time**.

### Meaning of terms:
- **I** → electric current (Amperes, A)  
- **Q** → electric charge (Coulombs, C)  
- **t** → time (seconds, s)  
- **dQ/dt** → derivative of charge with respect to time  

---

## ❓ Where does this formula come from?

Electric current is defined as:

> the amount of electric charge flowing per unit time.

If a small amount of charge ΔQ flows during a small time interval Δt:

I = ΔQ / Δt

When the charge changes continuously with time, we use calculus and replace:

ΔQ / Δt → dQ / dt

So the formula becomes:

I = dQ / dt

---

## ❓ Why do we use this formula here?

The problem gives charge as a function of time:

Q(t) = 5t² + 5

Since charge changes with time, we calculate current by taking the derivative of Q(t).

---

## 🔢 Solution

### 1. Given equation

Q(t) = 5t² + 5

---

### 2. Differentiate the equation

dQ/dt = 10t

So:

I(t) = 10t

---

### 3. Substitute t = 3 s

I = 10 × 3

---

### 4. Calculate

I = 30 A

---

## ✅ Final Answer

👉 I = 30 A

---

## 🎤 Short Explanation

> Electric current is the rate of change of charge with time.  
> We use the formula I = dQ/dt because the charge changes continuously.  
> After differentiating Q(t) = 5t² + 5, we get I(t) = 10t.  
> At t = 3 seconds, the current is 30 amperes.
