# Optimization Problem Solution  

## **5. Inelastic Collision Solution**  

---  

## **Problem**  

A **70 kg runner** moving at **3 m/s** jumps onto a **140 kg stationary cart**.  

👉 Find:  
1. The **final speed** of the system  
2. Whether **kinetic energy is conserved**  

---  

# **Step 1: Understand the Physics (What kind of collision?)**  

The runner **jumps onto the cart and stays on it**  

👉 This is a **perfectly inelastic collision**  

---  

## 🧠 WHAT does this imply?  

- Objects move together after collision  
- **Momentum is conserved**  
- **Kinetic energy is NOT conserved**  

---  

# **Step 2: Apply Momentum Conservation**  

Before collision:  

- Runner is moving  
- Cart is at rest  

$$
p_{\text{initial}} = m_1 v_1 + m_2 v_2
$$  

---  

Since the cart is at rest:  

$$
v_2 = 0
$$  

So:  

$$
p_{\text{initial}} = m_1 v_1
$$  

---  

After collision:  

$$
p_{\text{final}} = (m_1 + m_2)v_f
$$  

---  

## Write conservation equation:  

$$
m_1 v_1 = (m_1 + m_2)v_f
$$  

---  

## 🧠 WHY does this work?  

- No external horizontal forces  
- Total momentum must remain constant  

---  

# **Step 3: Solve for Final Velocity**  

$$
v_f = \frac{m_1 v_1}{m_1 + m_2}
$$  

---  

## Substitute values:  

$$
v_f = \frac{70 \cdot 3}{70 + 140}
$$  

---  

## Simplify:  

$$
v_f = \frac{210}{210}
$$  

$$
v_f = 1 \, \text{m/s}
$$  

---  

## ✅ RESULT (1)  

👉 Final speed = **1 m/s**  

---  

# **Step 4: Check Kinetic Energy**  

---  

## Initial kinetic energy:  

$$
E_i = \frac{1}{2} m_1 v_1^2
$$  

$$
E_i = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 70 \cdot 3^2
$$  

$$
E_i = 35 \cdot 9 = 315 \, \text{J}
$$  

---  

## Final kinetic energy:  

$$
E_f = \frac{1}{2} (m_1 + m_2) v_f^2
$$  

$$
E_f = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 210 \cdot 1^2
$$  

$$
E_f = 105 \, \text{J}
$$  

---  

# **Step 5: Compare Energies**  

👉 Initial: **315 J**  
👉 Final: **105 J**  

---  

## 🧠 WHAT does this mean?  

Energy has **decreased**  

👉 Lost energy is transformed into:  
- heat  
- sound  
- internal deformation  

---  

## ✅ RESULT (2)  

👉 Kinetic energy is **NOT conserved**  

---  

# **🔥 FINAL SUMMARY**  

- Momentum is conserved → used to find final speed  
- Inelastic collision → energy is lost  

👉 Final speed = **1 m/s**  
👉 Kinetic energy is **not conserved**
