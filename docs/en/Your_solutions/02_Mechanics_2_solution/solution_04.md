# Optimization Problem Solution  

## **4. Energy & Momentum Solution**  

---  

## **Problem**  

A **0.5 kg** block slides down a frictionless track from a height of **3.0 m**.  
At the bottom, it collides and sticks to a **1.5 kg** block at rest.  

👉 Find the **speed of the combined mass just after the collision**  

---  

# **Step 1: Understand the Physics (What is happening?)**  

This problem consists of **two different physical processes**:  

1. **Sliding down → Energy Conservation**  
2. **Collision → Momentum Conservation**  

---  

## 🧠 WHY do we separate them?  

Because:  

- During motion → **no energy loss** → use **mechanical energy**  
- During collision → **energy is NOT conserved** (objects stick)  
- BUT momentum is **always conserved**  

👉 So we must solve in **two steps**  

---  

# **Step 2: Find the Speed Before Collision**  

At the top:  

$$
E = mgh
$$  

At the bottom:  

$$
E = \frac{1}{2}mv^2
$$  

---  

## Apply conservation of energy:  

$$
mgh = \frac{1}{2}mv^2
$$  

---  

## 🧠 WHY does mass cancel?  

- Same object before and after  
- \(m\) appears on both sides  

👉 So motion depends only on **height**, not mass  

---  

## Solve for velocity:  

$$
v = \sqrt{2gh}
$$  

---  

## Substitute values:  

$$
v = \sqrt{2 \cdot 9.8 \cdot 3.0}
$$  

---  

## Calculation:  

$$
v = \sqrt{58.8}
$$  

$$
v \approx 7.67 \, \text{m/s}
$$  

---  

## ✅ INTERMEDIATE RESULT  

👉 Speed just before collision ≈ **7.67 m/s**  

---  

# **Step 3: Analyze the Collision**  

The block **sticks** to the second block  

👉 This is a **perfectly inelastic collision**  

---  

## 🧠 KEY IDEA:  

- Momentum is conserved  
- Kinetic energy is **NOT conserved**  

---  

# **Step 4: Apply Momentum Conservation**  

Before collision:  

$$
p_{\text{initial}} = m_1 v
$$  

After collision:  

$$
p_{\text{final}} = (m_1 + m_2)v_f
$$  

---  

## Write the equation:  

$$
m_1 v = (m_1 + m_2)v_f
$$  

---  

## 🧠 WHY this works?  

- No external horizontal forces  
- Total momentum must stay constant  

---  

# **Step 5: Solve for Final Velocity**  

$$
v_f = \frac{m_1 v}{m_1 + m_2}
$$  

---  

## Substitute values:  

$$
v_f = \frac{0.5 \cdot 7.67}{0.5 + 1.5}
$$  

---  

## Simplify:  

$$
v_f = \frac{3.835}{2.0}
$$  

$$
v_f \approx 1.92 \, \text{m/s}
$$  

---  

## ✅ RESULT  

👉 Final speed of the combined mass:  

$$
v_f \approx 1.92 \, \text{m/s}
$$  

---  

# **🔥 FINAL SUMMARY**  

- Height determines initial speed via **energy conservation**  
- Collision reduces speed due to increased mass  
- Momentum determines final velocity  

👉 Initial speed ≈ **7.67 m/s**  
👉 Final speed ≈ **1.92 m/s**
