# Optimization Problem Solution

## **7. Dynamics with Friction Solution**

---

## **Problem**

A 5 kg block is placed on a 10 kg block.  

* A horizontal force of **45 N** is applied to the 10 kg block  
* The 5 kg block is **tied to the wall** * The coefficient of kinetic friction between all surfaces is **0.2**

👉 Find the acceleration of the **10 kg block**

---

# **Step 1: Understand the Physical Situation**

**What’s happening?**
* The **10 kg block is pulled to the right**.
* The **5 kg block stays fixed** (because it's tied).
* So the 10 kg block **slides under the 5 kg block**.

### 🧠 WHY is friction important here?
Because the motion is opposed by **two friction forces**:
1. Between **10 kg block and ground** ($f_1$)
2. Between **5 kg block and 10 kg block** ($f_2$)

---

# **Step 2: Identify All Forces on the 10 kg Block**

### 👉 Forces acting:
1. **Applied force:** $F = 45\text{ N}$
2. **Friction with ground:** $f_1 = \mu N_1$
3. **Friction from top block:** $f_2 = \mu N_2$

---

# **Step 3: Calculate Normal Forces**

### 🔽 Normal from ground:
The ground supports **both masses**:
$$N_1 = (10 + 5)g$$
$$N_1 = 15 \cdot 9.8 = 147\text{ N}$$

### 🔽 Normal between blocks:
Only the top block contributes:
$$N_2 = 5g = 49\text{ N}$$

> **Note:** Ground feels both blocks, but the top interface only feels the top block.

---

# **Step 4: Calculate Friction Forces**

Using the formula $f = \mu N$:

* **Ground friction:** $f_1 = 0.2 \cdot 147 = 29.4\text{ N}$
* **Top friction:** $f_2 = 0.2 \cdot 49 = 9.8\text{ N}$

---

# **Step 5: Apply Newton’s Second Law**

$$F_{\text{net}} = m \cdot a$$

### Net force calculation:
$$F_{\text{net}} = F - f_1 - f_2$$
$$F_{\text{net}} = 45 - 29.4 - 9.8 = 5.8\text{ N}$$

---

# **Step 6: Solve for Acceleration**

$$a = \frac{F_{\text{net}}}{m}$$

### Substitute values:
$$a = \frac{5.8}{10}$$
**$$a = 0.58\text{ m/s}^2$$**

---

## ✅ RESULT
👉 Acceleration of the 10 kg block is **$0.58\text{ m/s}^2$**.

---

# **🔥 FINAL SUMMARY**
* Two friction forces oppose motion.
* Both ground and top interface frictions are subtracted from the applied force.
* Final acceleration $\approx$ **$0.58\text{ m/s}^2$**.
