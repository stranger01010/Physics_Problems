# Optimization Problem Solution  

## **6. Energy Dissipation Solution**  

---  

## **Problem**  

A tennis ball is dropped from a height of **2.0 m**.  

👉 After each bounce, it loses **30% of its mechanical energy**  

Find the height after the **second bounce**  

---  

# **Step 1: Understand the Physics (What is changing?)**  

The ball loses energy at every bounce  

---  

## 🧠 KEY IDEA:  

- Mechanical energy is proportional to height  

$$
E = mgh
$$  

👉 Since \(m\) and \(g\) are constant:  

$$
E \propto h
$$  

---  

## 🧠 WHY is this useful?  

It means:  

👉 Instead of tracking energy, we can directly track **height**  

---  

# **Step 2: Determine Remaining Energy Fraction**  

The ball loses **30%** of its energy after each bounce  

---  

## 🧠 WHY is the remaining energy \(0.7\)?  

Think in percentages:  

- Total energy = **100%**  
- Lost energy = **30%**  

👉 Remaining energy:  

$$
100\% - 30\% = 70\%
$$  

Convert to decimal:  

$$
70\% = 0.7
$$  

---  

## 🧠 WHAT does this mean physically?  

After each bounce:  

👉 The ball keeps only **70% of its previous energy**  

Since energy is proportional to height:  

👉 It also keeps **70% of its previous height**  

---  

# **Step 3: First Bounce**  

Initial height:  

$$
h_0 = 2.0 \, \text{m}
$$  

---  

After first bounce:  

$$
h_1 = 0.7 \cdot h_0
$$  

$$
h_1 = 0.7 \cdot 2.0
$$  

$$
h_1 = 1.4 \, \text{m}
$$  

---  

## ✅ INTERMEDIATE RESULT  

👉 Height after first bounce = **1.4 m**  

---  

# **Step 4: Second Bounce**  

Apply the same energy loss again:  

$$
h_2 = 0.7 \cdot h_1
$$  

$$
h_2 = 0.7 \cdot 1.4
$$  

$$
h_2 = 0.98 \, \text{m}
$$  

---  

## Alternative (direct method):  

$$
h_2 = h_0 \cdot (0.7)^2
$$  

$$
h_2 = 2.0 \cdot 0.49
$$  

$$
h_2 = 0.98 \, \text{m}
$$  

---  

## 🧠 WHY does this work?  

Each bounce multiplies energy by **0.7**  

👉  Two bounces → multiply twice → $(0.7^2)$

---  

## ✅ RESULT  

👉 Height after second bounce:  

$$
h_2 \approx 0.98 \, \text{m}
$$  

---  

# **🔥 FINAL SUMMARY**  

- Losing **30% energy** means **70% remains (0.7)**  
- Energy and height are proportional  
- Heights decrease geometrically  

👉 Final height ≈ **0.98 m**
